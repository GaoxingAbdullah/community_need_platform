Housing Assistance

Finding and Affording Housing

Finding and affording housing can be a challenge. Here are some resources that can help:

Local Organizations

  • Habitat for Humanity

    Habitat for Humanity is a non-profit organization that helps families build and improve places to call home. We build, renovate and repair homes using volunteer labor and donations.

    Learn More

  • Community Action Agency

    Community Action Agencies are private and public nonprofit organizations that were established as part of the Economic Opportunity Act of 1964 to fight America's War on Poverty.

    Learn More

  • Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)

    The Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) is a Cabinet department in the Executive branch of the United States federal government.

    Learn More

Government Programs

  • Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program

    The Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher program is a federal government program for assisting low-income families, the elderly, and the disabled to afford decent, safe, and sanitary housing in the private market.

    Learn More

  • Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)

    The Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) helps keep families safe and healthythrough financial assistance and education for energy bills. LIHEAP is a grant that offers assistance in the form of cash or crisis grants to eligible households to help pay for heating and cooling costs.

    Learn More

  • Public Housing

    Public housing is rental housing for low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities. Public housing is owned and managed by public housing agencies (PHAs). These agencies use federal funds from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) to provide safe and decent rental housing to low-income families.

    Learn More
