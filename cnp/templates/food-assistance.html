Food Assistance

Overview

Food assistance programs provide food to low-income individuals and families. These programs include, but are not limited to, food stamps (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)), Women, Infants and Children (WIC) and school meal programs.

Programs

  • Food Stamps (SNAP)

    The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) provides food assistance to low-income individuals and families. Eligible individuals can use SNAP benefits to purchase food at participating retailers.

    Learn More

  • Women, Infants and Children (WIC)

    The Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) program provides food assistance to pregnant and postpartum women, infants, and children up to age 5. WIC also provides nutrition education and breastfeeding support.

    Learn More

  • School Meal Programs

    School meal programs provide free or reduced-price meals to eligible students. These programs include the National School Lunch Program and the School Breakfast Program.
